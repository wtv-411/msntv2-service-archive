Save e-mail draft
Interrupted while writing an e-mail? You can answer the doorbell, surf online, sign off – whatever you need to do – and finish writing the e-mail later. Here's how:

  1. On the Write e-mail page, choose Save as Draft.
  2. When you want to finish the e-mail, choose Folders from anywhere in Mail.
  3. Choose Drafts to see a list of your e-mail drafts.
  4. Select the draft you want to finish, and start writing.
Use e-mail Quicknames
Tired of remembering long e-mail addresses? Set up Quicknames for your contacts.
  1. From Mail, choose Address book.
  2. Select the entry you want to edit.
  3. Add or edit the Quickname.
  4. The next time you want to write to that person, just type the Quickname in the To: line.
  5. When you send the e-mail and choose Send, the person's complete e-mail address will replace the Quickname.
The next time you want to write your cousin Phil, instead of typing the whole address (philsmith269@mymailbox.com), you can just type 'phil.'
Find addresses fast
Want to look up an e-mail address fast?
  1. On the Write e-mail page, just choose To: to bring up your list of contacts.
  2. Check the box next to the person you'd like to write to, and choose Continue. (The e-mail address will automatically be inserted.)
  3. Type your e-mail message and choose Send.
(Note: You can also look up addresses by choosing Address book.)
Send photos in e-mail
Have some cool vacation or party photos you'd like to share with others? Here's how to e-mail them:
  1. When you're writing an e-mail, choose Insert Photos.
  2. Select the location of the photos you want to share: e-mail, an album on your player, a memory card, or a folder on your home network.
  3. Choose Insert Photos again.
  4. Choose a photo quality (Full for the original quality, or Friendly to send a file that's good for viewing, but not for printing)
  5. Choose Send.
Now everyone can see your pictures and share in the fun.
Respond to e-mail with one click
When you're reading an e-mail and want to write back quickly, just choose Reply on the right side of your screen. You'll see a blank e-mail form with the recipient's e-mail address already filled in.

If others were included in the original e-mail, you can include them too in your response by choosing Reply all.

Attach the original e-mail
Want to respond to something your friend said in her last e-mail? You can add a copy of the original message when you Reply.

  1. From your Inbox, choose Settings.
  2. Choose Include original e-mail.
  3. Place a check-mark next to "Include original e-mail …" and choose Save Changes.

Now both you and your friend will remember what you're replying to.

Cut, copy and paste text
Want to copy words from a Web site and then paste them into an e-mail you're writing? Or maybe delete some words from the e-mail you’re about to forward to a friend? You can do both with the Cut, Copy & Paste features.

Just select the first word in the text you want to cut or copy, then hold down the Shift key and Right Arrow key until you’ve highlighted the words you want to use. Then:

  • To cut the text, press the CTRL key and the X key at the same time.
  • To copy it, press the CTRL key and the C key at the same time.
Once you've cut or copied some text, you can paste it into a new location. Move your cursor to the spot where you'd like to insert the text, and then press the CTRL key and the V key at the same time.

You'll be able to move sentences and paragraphs around your e-mail, or copy sections of Web pages — like a new recipe — without all the retyping.

Check your spelling
Want to check your spelling before you send that e-mail? Our Spellchecker tool helps you look for misspelled words in the body of an e-mail message and correct them. You can also add words to your dictionary, such as names of people or places that are spelled correctly, but aren't in the dictionary.

Here's how it works:

  1. From your Inbox, choose Write e-mail.
  2. Write your e-mail message, and choose the Check Spelling link on the right.
  3. If no spelling errors are found, you will get a message confirming this.
  4. If spelling errors are found, you will see the unrecognized word highlighted.
    • If the unrecognized word is acceptable, you can choose Add to Dictionary, Ignore, or Ignore all.
    • If the word is indeed a spelling error, you can choose from a list of alternate spellings at the bottom of the page. The first word on the list is automatically highlighted; to accept this spelling, choose Change or Change all. To select another spelling in the list, move your yellow selection box to the word you wish to use. Then, choose Change or Change all.

Enjoy and happy spelling!

Cut, copy and paste Web pages
You may already know that you can share fun Web pages or interesting articles with friends by pressing the Menu key and choosing Send to send the URL.

But what if the article or Web page changes before your friend has had a chance to read it? You can cut or copy an entire Web page and paste it into an e-mail, so that the recipient will see just what you saw when you sent it.

To highlight a page all at once, press the CTRL+A keys. Then choose to copy (CTRL + C) or cut the selection (CTRL + X) and paste (CTRL + V) as you normally would when editing within an e-mail.

Create a group e-mail address
Do you e-mail the same group of people often? Save time by adding individual addresses under a single Group Quickname.

For example, you might create a quickname called "MyFamily" for your relatives, or "HobbyFriends" for folks who share your interests.

Then, when you write your next group e-mail, instead of typing in all the individual addresses in the "To" field, just type the Quickname of the group you want.

To get started making a Group Quickname in Mail, just choose Address Book, and then select Add a Group.

Paste a Web page in e-mail
You may already know that you can share Web pages with friends by pressing the Menu key and choosing Send (this sends the Web address).

But did you know you can also copy an entire Web page and paste it into an e-mail? To copy and send the contents of a Web page:

  1. Press the CTRL+A keys to highlight the contents of the Web page.
  2. Press CTRL + C to copy the selection.
  3. In the body of your e-mail, press CTRL+V to paste the contents.

Check your system's health
Does your MSN TV 2 player suffer from any of the following symptoms?
  • Web pages take a long time to display.
  • Only part of a Web page appears on the screen.
  • Keyboard performance is slow.
If your player shows any of these symptoms, it's time to check its health:
  1. From the sign-in page, press Favs on your keyboard. The System Information page appears.
  2. Look at the "System Health" category. It should say "OK." If your player's system health is "Poor," choose Reset System to turn your player off and back on. This should restore the system to full working order.
Improve the way your Web pages look
Web Accelerator for Dial-up is a feature that speeds up Web-page display when you connect to MSN TV using a phone line. Web Accelerator makes pages display more quickly, but with a slight loss of image quality. When it's turned off, Web pages take longer to load but look somewhat better. If you want to improve the appearance of Web pages, try turning off Web Accelerator (Web Accelerator is by default on):
  1. Choose Settings from the sign-in page.
  2. Select Choose connection settings.
  3. Choose Web Accelerator options for dial-up.
  4. Remove the checkmark next to "Web Accelerator for Dial-up."
  5. Choose Save Changes.
To turn Web Accelerator back on, follow the steps above and check the box next to "Web Accelerator for Dial-up."

  2. Scroll to the bottom of the Help Index and choose View your system information. (Shortcut: From the sign-in page, press the Favs key on your keyboard.)
  3. Choose Connection History. On the Connection History page, you'll see a list that shows what happened when your player dialed MSN TV, including the date and time you connected, the access number that was dialed, and the connection rate.
Turn on audible dialing
If you're having trouble connecting to the MSN TV service, the first thing you should do is turn on audible dialing and listen as your player dials. When audible dialing is turned on, you can determine whether your player is dialing correctly (you will hear standard modem noise). If there's a dialing problem, you may hear a recorded operator message.

To turn on audible dialing:

  1. Choose Settings from the sign-in page.
  2. Select Choose connection settings.
  3. Choose Dialing options and troubleshooting.
  4. Select Choose whether to listen to dialing.
  5. Check the box next to "Listen to dialing," and then choose Save Changes.

Clear your cookies
Though there are lots of uses for cookies on the Web, most sites use them to track info such as your name, prior purchases, etc. so they can offer you personalized information. (Example: Amazon.com) You can clear your cookies by following these steps:

  1. From the home page, choose Settings.
  2. Choose Web appearance and security.
  3. Choose Delete cookies.
Create a music song list
Did you know you can use your MSN TV 2 player as your very own jukebox, playing just the songs you want, in the order you want? To get started:
  1. Go to Music.
  2. Choose Song List.
  3. Choose Add Songs.

Note: You can add songs in MP3 or WMA formats, from any networked PC or compatible device.

Choose how you play your music
Are you the methodical type who listens to songs in a specific order, or do you like to throw caution to the wind and mix things up? Now, you can do both.

After you create a song list in Music, you can decide to play the contents in order (choose Loop) or at random (choose Shuffle).

Listen to music you bought online
Want to play music you purchased online? If your MSN TV 2 player is connected to a home network, you can play back Windows Media-compatible music you purchased (from MSN Music, Napster or other services) from a networked PC.

Choose what you see while listening to music
When you play music on MSN TV 2, you get to choose what appears in your display: virtual fireworks, cover art from the album being played, or photos from your screensaver.

To change the images you see when playing music, go to Music and choose Settings. Select what you want to see and choose Save Changes.

Listen to 'local' music
Imagine listening to your favorite radio station, but without the commercials. Sound good? Then give MSN Local Sounds a try. (This music is programmed by MSN and not affiliated with any radio stations.)
  1. From the home page, go to Music.
  2. Choose MSN Radio.
  3. Choose MSN Local Sounds.
  4. Select your City.
  5. Choose a station to play, or choose Add Stations to add a station to your My Stations list.
Shop for music
Heard a tune you like on MSN Radio? Why not add it to your personal music collection?

When a song is playing, you'll see the artist and album cover of the song on the screen. Just choose the Buy CD link next to the album cover to get started shopping.

Personalize your music
Want to create your own list of favorite radio stations?

From the home page, just choose Music. Under My Stations, choose Edit/More to get started.

The next time you want to get to My Stations, just choose Edit/More again.

Play music in the background
Want to listen to a little music while you read e-mail and surf?

While your favorite station is playing, choose the Hide button. This hides the Media Player window and lets you keep listening to music while you read e-mail, surf the Web, instant message, etc.

To return to the Media Player page, press Player on your keyboard.

What on earth does :-) mean?
:-) is a smile, online style. ;-) is a wink, and :)* is a kiss.

These little pictures are called emoticons, and people use them to illustrate different emotions in e-mail and Chat.

So tilt your head to the left a little and start :-)ing online.

Find a chat room
Want to meet someone special online? Discuss your favorite TV show or hobby with others?

Choose Chat from the home page to go to the main Chat page. Then, choose a chat service and scroll down the list of chat rooms until you find one that interests you. Or, if you know the name of a room you like, choose Go to Room, type the name of the chat room, and choose Go.

Change your chat nickname
A little privacy can be a good thing when you’re chatting.

If you don’t want to use your MSN TV user name when you chat, you can add a new user, and sign in as that user when you want to chat.

To learn how to add a user, choose Help from the home page, select the Account category, and then choose Adding a user to your account.

Block a buddy
Want to stop a buddy from contacting you?
  1. From your home page, choose Messenger from the scrolling list at the bottom of the page.
  2. Choose Edit a contact.
  3. Select the name of the person you want to block.
  4. Choose Continue.
  5. Select Remove from contact list.
  6. Choose Save Changes.

Voilà! Now this person can no longer see when you're online or send you messages.

Get help while you're chatting
Got a question while chatting? Each IRC chat service on MSN TV operates a little differently.

If you're not sure how a chat feature works, first choose Help from the upper-left corner of any Chat screen. If you don't find the info you need there, try the help room for the chat service you're using. Choose Go to Room and then type help. If that doesn't work, choose Go to Room and then type the name of the chat service.

Add a buddy
To add someone to your Messenger buddy list, just follow these steps:
  1. From your home page, choose Messenger from the scrolling list at the bottom of the page.
  2. Choose Add a contact and type the e-mail address of the person you want to add.
  3. Choose Add.
