Shape up in 2005
Sites to help you slim down for the New Year
By Heather Moylan
First, the bad news: 1) 64% of Americans are overweight, according to the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. 2) The most popular New Year’s resolution is to lose weight, but few have long-term success. 3) Most dieters ultimately gain back their lost pounds (and a few extra).

The bottom line is, if you want to drop a few sizes, it's not going to be easy.

But enough of the downers. Here’s something good: With easy tips and tools like calorie calculators, the Web can help you do a lot of the planning work, if not the workout itself.

Getting a plan
Atkins, South Beach, the Zone, Weight Watchers — you’ve likely heard of them all but don’t know what’s involved (can the crazy all-meat myth be true?) and, more importantly, if any would fit your lifestyle.

MSN Health's "Which Diet is Best for You?" quiz tackles the guesswork. Fill out the multiple-choice questionnaire about your shopping habits, ideal vacation, and other interests, and they come right back with the answers. Are you impulsive? Then opt for something less restrictive, like Weight Watchers. Sweet-tooths, meanwhile, should avoid high-protein diets like Atkins. The site also links to more information and plan descriptions.

If you want a second opinion, do your own homework and read WebMD’s reviews of 17 different diet plans.

It also might be that no "plan" is perfect, and you’re better off marching, or jogging, to your own beat. There are sites to help with that strategy, too.

Ab & number crunching
Experts agree: Exercise is key to any successful weight-loss plan. A couch potato who cuts out carbs may have short-term success, but in the end, it’s a losing game — especially if he/she picks up the pasta again.

So you’ve got to get a move on. It can be as simple as a short walk in the neighborhood in the morning, taking the stairs instead of the elevator at work, or picking up the pace when you tour the mall.

To step it up, MSN Health’s The Right Buff has got guides like "Six Picks for Six Packs" and "Step by Step: Walking for Fitness" to assist with specific goals.

For the nuts and bolts of number crunching on exercising, Yahoo’s got a no-frills Physical Activity Overview which defines things like Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and your target heart rate. But there are also fun online tools to keep you from sweating the small stuff:

  • Body Fat Percentage — Enter gender, age, weight, height, and waist size to get the bottom line, and some advice.
  • Metabolism Calculator — How many calories do you burn every day? It depends on factors like your age, weight, and activity level.
  • Rate Your Energy — If you’re jacking up the caffeine to go that extra mile during the week, you’re on the wrong track.
  • Fast Food Choices — What to order and what to avoid at Wendy’s, Subway, and more pit-stops.
  • Activity Database — From aerobics to yoga, golf to T’ai Chi, this tool walks you through the first steps, and the target areas you’ll be working.
  • Dessert Wizard — Last but not least, there’s the final course — which many of us hate to skip. This calculator makes sense of the sweets, calculating how much time you’ll need to run or nap, say, to burn off that brownie or banana split. (Warning: It may be a buzz kill.)
