Finding favorites fast
By Erfert Fenton
If you visit a lot of Web pages, chances are you've saved some of them as favorites so you can return to them quickly whenever you like. But if you have lots of favorites, it can be hard finding a particular one â€“ you may have to search through a long list, or multiple favorites folders. Here are some tips that can help navigating through favorites easier.

For a quick intro on saving favorites, choose this link: Introduction to favorites

Tip: In addition to Web pages, you can save MSN TV pages as favorites. To get to your favorite photo album in a flash, for example, save it as a favorite. For even quicker access, assign it a Fav # shortcut key on your keyboard.

Organizing your favorites
When you're looking at a page and want to save it as a favorite, just press the Save Fav key on your keyboard. The Save As a Favorite page appears, offering a number of options.

If you choose the Save button, the page will automatically be saved in the favorites list (this list is also called the Main folder). If you like, you can create an additional folder, and then put the favorite into that folder by pressing Save Fav. To learn more about creating and organizing folders, choose this link: Organizing your favorites

Tip: In addition to placing a favorite in any folder, you can change the favorite's name before you save it. Just press Save Fav, highlight the name of the new favorite, and then type any name you like. (If you decide to rename the favorite later, press Favs, choose Organize, choose Edit, and follow the instructions there.)

Getting around
If you have dozens of favorites in your favorites list, it might take you a while to scroll to the favorite you want. Never fear! There's a much quicker way. Just type the first letter of a favorite's name, and you'll jump to the first page that starts with that letter. (If there are no pages that start with the letter you typed, you'll jump to the first folder that starts with that letter.)

Typing the first letter of a favorite's name is a great shortcut, but there's an even quicker way to get to your favorite pages. Let's say you check the Yeti Watchers News page every day, and you've already used up your allotment of Fav # shortcut keys. Since you know you're going to go to that page every day, you can rename the favorite "AAA Yeti Watchers News" to place it at the top of your favorites list. Here's how:

  1. Press the Favs key to go to your favorites list. (If the favorite you want to rename is in a folder, choose that folder to open it.)
  2. Choose the Organize button (or Organize Folder if you've opened a folder.)
  3. Choose Edit.
  4. Select the favorite you want to rename.
  5. Choose the Edit button.
  6. Type a new name for the favorite.
  7. Choose Save.
In addition to renaming favorites or folders, you can use Organize to add or delete folders, remove favorites, or move favorites from one folder to another.

Tip: To quickly zip to the top of the Favorites page, press the Jump to Top key. To zip to the bottom of the page, press Ctrl+Page Down.

A message from our sponsor
When you go to your favorites list, you'll sometimes see a message that tells you your favorites list is being updated. That's because backup copies of your favorites are stored by MSN TV. That way, if your MSN TV 2 player malfunctions, you won't lose your list of favorites, which may have taken you hours to create. (Another bonus: Because your favorites are stored by MSN TV, you can see your list of favorites on more than one player, as long as you sign in with the same account name you used when you created the favorites list.)
