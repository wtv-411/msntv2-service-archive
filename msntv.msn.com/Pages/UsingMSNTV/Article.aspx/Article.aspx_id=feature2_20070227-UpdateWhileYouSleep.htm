Update while you sleep
Set the time MSN TV checks for and installs updates
By Erfert Fenton
Spring is here, and the days are getting longer. However, they're not so long that you can afford to spend up to a few hours waiting for your MSN TV 2 player to accept the latest MSN TV update. (Update speeds vary, depending on the size of the update and how your player connects to MSN TV.)

When an update is available, an update notice will appear when you turn on your player. If you don't want to wait around while the update is installed, you can choose Update Later and get on with your life. However, you can only choose Update Later a few times; after that, your player will go ahead and accept the update, and you won't be able to use your player while the update is progressing. (Believe me, you'll want to check out the latest update as soon as possible, as it includes great new features.)

Don't worry — you don't have to waste any time waiting around for updates. You can set your player to accept updates at a time when you're not using it, such as in the middle of the night when you're asleep. Using Automatic Update, you can tell your player to accept MSN TV updates at a time you choose. Here's how:

  1. Choose Settings from the home page.
  2. Choose Automatic update.
  3. On the page that appears, select the checkbox next to "Check for and install updates."
  4. Select the box to the right of "Begin checks around," and then choose a time (scroll to see additional times).
  5. Choose Save Changes.
Tip: While you're on the Automatic Update page, you can also tell your player how often to check for new mail.

There. Now you can use your player during the day, get some shuteye at night, and wake up to a fresh new update — all without wasting a minute.

Tip: Once you're received an update, check your e-mail for a message that describes the improvements provided by the update.
